Pick a date

A case worth exploring

http://esa-digital.herokuapp.com/health03/health-conditions

Research has shown that people have variable mental models of when certain conditions first began, e.g. Three years ago, 2011, etc.

We should allow people to continue to describe these dates in the manner that is most natural to their mental model.

However — we know that legacy systems require the dates in a specific DD MM YY format. Whilst there may be temptation to include a date picker as a solution to a system problem, we should instead look to do the hard work to keep it simple for the user.

We could attempt to identify key words or phrases that are entered and automatically convert those to a suitable date format in the backend

Example, three years ago would become 01 11 2013.

Part of this will be challenging the specificity of the dates required, so that we can automatically default day to the first of the month when required.

Date pickers (calendar controls)

We have not seen/validated a user need for this.

Outstanding questions

  • Have we seen/validated a user need (not a want) for this?
  • Are there instances where the user will not always know a specific date?
  • Will the user have a relative mental model on when the date is, eg next friday, or the first day of next month?
  • Can the interaction can be made simplier/quicker with a picker?
  • Does manual input create errors?

A word from GDS

Calendar controls are only appropriate for near past or future dates, where the day of the week is relevant. Their main use case is for appointment booking.

If you also need to show information like availability, embed the calendar in the page and make it large enough for the information to be readable.

A calendar control that depends on JavaScript should never be the only input option.

https://www.gov.uk/service-manual/user-centred-design/resources/patterns/dates.html
