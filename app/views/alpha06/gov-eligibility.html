2. Eligibility

You may be able to get Employment and Support Allowance (ESA) if you have an illness or disability which stops you from working and you:

  • are under state pension age
  • are not getting Jobseekers Allowance
  • are unemployed
  • are self-employed
  • work for an employer but cannot get Statutory Sick Pay (SSP)
  • were getting SSP which has now stopped
  • are a student who is getting Disability Living Allowance or Personal Independence Payment
  • have a valid fit note from your doctor for any period of illness after the first seven days

You will not be able to get ESA if you have savings and investments over £16,000 and have not worked in the last 2-3 years.

Terminal illness

If you have a terminal illness apply as normal. But, your claim will be dealt with faster.

Repeat claims

In most cases, if you were found to be capable of doing some work when you claimed before, you will not be able to get ESA, unless:

  • your current condition has got a lot worse
  • you’re claiming with a new condition
