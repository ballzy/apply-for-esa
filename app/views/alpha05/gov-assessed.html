3. How you're assessed

  • You’ll get a questionnaire soon after your claim to find out how your illness or disability affects you.
  • You’ll get a letter with either a decision on your capability to work or an invitation to a meet face-to-face
  • If you are invited to a face-to-face you’ll be asked more about how your condition affects you. It lasts about an hour and you can take someone with you.
  • You’ll get a decision letter about your ability to work within 4 weeks. You can be:
    • placed in the support group – your ESA will be at least £125.05 a week and you do not have to go to interviews
    • placed in a work related activity group – your ESA will be at least £102.15 a week and you must go to regular interviews with an adviser who can help you prepare for work when you’re able to
    • found to be fit for work and payment of ESA will stop

Once you have a decision on your claim, you no longer need to send in any fit notes, unless you think the decision is wrong and want to appeal.
