3. How you're assessed

  • You’ll get a questionnaire soon after your claim to find out how your illness or disability affects you. If you don’t return the questionnaire, payment of ESA could stop.
  • You’ll get a letter with either, a decision that you are not capable of work, or an invitation to meet face-to-face.
  • If you are invited to a face-to-face you’ll be asked more about how your condition affects you. It lasts about an hour and you can take someone with you. If you don’t go to the face-to-face meeting, payment of ESA could stop.
  • You’ll get a decision letter about your ability to work within 4 weeks. You can be:
    • placed in the support group and do not have to go to any interviews – most people get £125.05 a week
    • placed in a work related activity group and must go to regular interviews with an adviser who can help you prepare for work when you’re able to – most people get £102.15 a week
    • found to be fit for work and payment of ESA will stop

The amount you get can be higher or lower depending on your personal circumstances.

Once you have a decision on your claim, you no longer need to send in any fit notes, unless you think the decision is wrong and want to appeal.
