2. Who can get ESA

You can get Employment and Support Allowance (ESA) if you have an illness or disability which affects your ability to work and:

  • are under state pension age - check your state pension age
  • are not getting Jobseeker's Allowance
  • have a fit note from your doctor for any period of illness after the first 7 days
  • are unemployed, self-employed, or work for an employer but can’t get Statutory Sick Pay (SSP)
  • were getting SSP which has now stopped
  • are a student getting Disability Living Allowance or Personal Independence Payment

If you claim ESA, you could get National Insurance credits that count towards your State Pension.

Terminal illness

If you have a terminal illness and less than six months to live you still have to claim, but will get their money much faster.

Repeat claims

In most cases, if you have claimed ESA before and were found to be capable of doing some work, you will not be able to get ESA unless either:

  • your current condition has got a lot worse
  • you’re claiming with a new condition
